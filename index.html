Common Questions

How many photos do I need?
While our system can process up to 1,500 photos, we recommend a minimum of six different images for optimal results. The more diverse the photos, the richer the memorial experience will be.
What types of photos work best?
Clear photos that show the person in different settings and time periods work best. However, our technology can work with damaged or altered photos as well, using algorithmic sharpening to reveal details not visible in the original.
How long does the process take?
The entire process typically takes 2-3 weeks from receiving your kit to the final memorial creation. The memory collection sessions themselves usually require 3-5 hours spread across multiple days.
Who will see the final memorial?
You have complete control over who experiences the memorial. It can be shared at a funeral service, kept private for immediate family, or shared with a wider circle of friends and relatives.
Is the experience emotionally difficult?
The process can be emotionally intense, but many users find it therapeutic. Our AI guide is designed to be supportive and sensitive, and you can pause or stop the process at any time.
What is a memorial tribute video?
A memorial tribute video is a beautiful and meaningful way to say goodbye to a friend or family member. Eulogy's Immersive Memorial Experiences go beyond traditional videos, helping you illustrate how a person lived and honor their memory. They provide an opportunity for people to get to know different sides of your loved one through the story of the photos you share.
How do I select photos for a memorial tribute?
When selecting photos, consider your loved one's life and the themes that honor and represent that person. Each photo should reflect one of these themes. Look for images that show hobbies, passions, interests, personality, childhood moments, relationships with family and friends, special moments like graduations or weddings, and favorite travel destinations.
What is Eulogy's process for creating memorials?
Our process begins with the Eulogy kit, which includes the guide disc and setup instructions. Once activated, the AI guide helps you through five key steps: 1) Photo Collection - uploading or scanning cherished photographs, 2) Memory Mapping - the AI asks gentle questions about each photo, 3) Environment Creation - transforming flat photographs into 3D spaces, 4) Audio Integration - adding music, ambient sounds, or voice recordings, and 5) Memorial Curation - arranging memories in a meaningful sequence.
How does the AI guide help with creating memorials?
Throughout the memorial creation process, your AI guide provides both emotional support and technical assistance, ensuring the experience is healing and meaningful. The guide helps you navigate through memories, asks thoughtful questions to help recall important details, and assists in creating a cohesive narrative of your loved one's life. The final memorial can be experienced privately or shared with family and friends during a service.
